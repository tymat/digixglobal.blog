Digix Scalability Research
Introduction
About DigixCore
DigixCore is the asset tokenization platform that enables the tokenization of real world assets through a set of business process rules that are enforced by the Ethereum Virtual Machine.
Proof of Provenance
Digix Proof of Provenance is a protocol for a transparent supply chain system that is essential to the Digix asset tokenization process by providing on-chain records of every action needed to be taken by different entities in order to tokenize physical assets into the public blockchain. For DigixCore, this is used to tokenize physical gold bars which are stored at a third party custodial vault into the Digix Gold Token (DGX). This process involves four classes of entities:
Administrator
This entity is responsible for initiating the asset tokenization process.
Vendor
This entity is responsible for the second step of the asset tokenization process where the sales receipt as well as photographs of the physical gold bars are uploaded into IPFS and the resulting IPFS hash is recorded on the Ethereum blockchain.
Custodian
This entity is responsible for the third step of the asset tokenization process where the parcel delivery receipt and physical bar tests (DUX test) documents are uploaded and recorded in a similar way as the Vendor step. This is the final step into tokenizing the physical gold bar into Digix Gold Tokens.
Auditor
This entity performs quarterly audits on the contents of the custodial vaults and uploads and records in a similar way as the Vendor and Custodian. Gold bars that fail the audit are explicitly marked as such and the DGX system enters into a fractional state until the physical bars that have failed the audits have been replaced.
Digix Gold Token
The Digix Gold Token (DGX) is a token on the Ethereum blockchain where one token represents 1 gram of physical gold divisible to 9 decimal points or 1 nanogram of gold. It is currently implemented as an ERC20 token with some special modifications from the base ERC20 standard.
Users can redeem physical gold in exchange for their tokens. To redeem a 100 gram physical gold bar a user would need to 'burn' (called a recast) 101 DGX tokens (1 DGX goes towards the fee which covers logistical and other costs)
In order to cover the costs involved with storing physical assets into a custodian's secure vaulting facility, performance of quarterly third party audits, and other costs, Digix charges demurrage and transaction fees.
Transaction Fees
DGX transfers between wallets will incur a 0.13% fee of the total amount sent. The collected fees are automatically transferred to a fee collector smart contract.
Demurrage Fees
Demurrage is used to cover the cost of storing and securing the physical gold bars at a third party custodial vault. Digix charges 0.60% of the total amount of gold held in each account and is calculated on a daily basis.
DigixDAO
DigixDAO is a smart contract enforced governance platform whose primary goal is to crowdsource and fund ideas that would help the growth and adoption of the DGX gold tokens. DigixDAO uses a standard ERC20 token called DGDs which are locked into the system by participants in order to become eligible participants in the governance platform.
DGD token holders participate in creating proposals and voting on proposals in exchange for getting rewarded with a portion of the DGX transaction and demurrage fees collected by the system.
Known Issues and Pain Points
Ethereum network costs
As DigixCore is implemented as a collection of Ethereum smart contracts running on the Ethereum public chain, every state changing transaction from the PoP tokenization process, wallet to wallet transfers, the collection of demurrage and transaction fees, and the conversion of DGX tokens for a physical gold bar must pay miners on the public chain some amount of ETH as "gas fees" to execute these transactions. As DGX tokens require some additional state storage writes for transaction fees and demurrage costs the transfers cost more than most standard ERC20 token transfers. This presents not only a major usability problem for DigixCore participants and DGX users, but also burdens the users with requiring them to obtain ETH in order to use DGX tokens.
Bulk PoP processing
DigixCore implements bulk processing functions on the smart contracts to facilitate the tokenization of multiple physical bars in a single transaction. While this currently works as designed we are still limited to processing about 20 bars per transaction due to the public chain's block gas limit. This severely hinders our ability to scale the process for mass commercial use.
Slow transaction throughput
As DGX tokens are ERC20 tokens on the public Ethereum blockchain our transaction throughput is limited by Ethereum's block gas limit and block generation times.
Centralized and decentralized exchange integration complexity
The non-standard behavior of DGX tokens presents a problem for both centralized (CEX) and decentralized exchanges (DEX).
Certain DEX platforms may fail to recognize the rolling deductions taking place on an account's DGX balance and therefore may cause some undesired behavior on the DEX platform including complete inaccessibility of funds.
Most centralized exchanges perform a 'sweep' of tokens from a user's deposit address into the exchange's hot wallet. This presents an increased cost of depositing DGX tokens into an exchange as two transaction fees must be deducted.
Proposed Solution
In order to eliminate the issues and pain points outlined above, it seems apparent that Digix must develop a separate blockchain system that is designed from the ground up to support various aspects of the DigixCore system (PoP process, demurrage fee accounting, transaction fees collection, exchange accounting integration) as primitives.
Ethereum network costs
With a dedicated chain users will only have to pay the standard DGX transaction fees. DigixCore full node validators will be paid some amount of DGX tokens for adding transactions on the ledger.
Bulk PoP Processing
PoP primitives can be executed at a low cost in a permissioned manner.
Slow transaction throughput
This problem should be alleviated in a dedicated chain as users do not have to share the blocks with other decentralized applications on the Ethereum public chain.
CEX and DEX integration complexity
Implementing a multi-signature/multi-account deposit system Digix can assign special designation to exchanges that would allow them to manage multiple sub-accounts that can automatically sweep customer deposits in and out of their hot wallet.
